# Vannevar Bush

American engineer, science administrator, and proto-computing visionary best known for the 1945 essay *As We May Think*, which described the [[entities/memex]] — a personal mechanical knowledge store with associative links between documents. The Memex is widely cited as a conceptual precursor to hypertext, the World Wide Web, and personal knowledge management systems.

## Notable contributions

- **The Memex (1945)**: imagined a desk-sized device storing books, records, and communications, navigable by associative trails between documents — see [[entities/memex]].
- **Associative trails**: the conceptual ancestor of hypertext links. A user-curated path through documents, persisting the connections the user finds important — see [[concepts/associative-trails]].

## Why he's load-bearing here

The [[sources/llm-wiki-pattern]] explicitly positions this whole project as a descendant of Bush's vision. Per that source, Bush identified the right problem (private, curated, link-rich knowledge stores) but couldn't solve **who maintains the trails**. LLMs make the maintenance free, which (in the framing of this wiki) is the unblock.
