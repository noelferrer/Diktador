# Memex

Hypothetical personal knowledge device proposed by [[entities/vannevar-bush]] in his 1945 essay *As We May Think*. Imagined as a desk containing microfilm of a person's books, records, and communications, navigable by user-curated associative links between documents. The Memex is the conceptual ancestor of hypertext, the web, and personal knowledge management.

## Core properties (per the original framing)

- **Personal** — owned and operated by one user, not a shared/public system.
- **Curated** — the user actively decides what enters the store and how documents relate.
- **Associative** — documents are linked by user-defined trails reflecting the user's own reasoning, not by predefined taxonomy.
- **Compounding** — value grows as more material is added and more trails are laid down.

## Relationship to this wiki

[[sources/llm-wiki-pattern]] frames the LLM Wiki pattern as a successor to the Memex. The framing: Bush specified the right artifact but the maintenance burden killed every serious attempt to build one. The web, the closest thing we got, is public and uncurated rather than private and curated. With LLMs the maintenance becomes free, so the original Memex vision becomes practical for the first time.

> "The part [Bush] couldn't solve was who does the maintenance. The LLM handles that." — [[sources/llm-wiki-pattern]]

## Open questions
